COVID-19 Web Applications

On December 31, 2019, the WHO China Country Office was informed of cases of pneumonia of unknown cause detected in Wuhan City, Hubei Province of China. Within days, 44 patients with pneumonia of unknown etiology were reported to WHO by Chinese authorities. Of these 44 cases, 11 were reported to be severely ill, an early indication that this disease was potentially more serious than common illnesses such as seasonal influenza. Fast-forward several months, millions have been infected, hundreds of thousands have died, and the global economy is in a state of distress unseen since the Great Depression. Despite these alarming statistics, many have risen to the challenge of better understanding this disease. Sites such as The New York Times, the Johns Hopkins Coronavirus Resource Center, and Our World in Data present information that serves to better understand both the current state and the spread of covid-19. Using the data made publicly available by these sites, I have created web applications to explore covid-19 data both within the US and around the world. The code behind these sites may be examined by clicking the Github button. Links to each of the sites are included further below.
